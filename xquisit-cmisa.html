The Canadian Marine Industries and Shipbuilding Association
Branding

The Canadian Marine Industries and Shipbuilding Association (CMISA) is a national, not-for-profit organization that drives and promotes innovation, strategic partnerships, and advocacy and advancement of their industry across the country.

In order to solidify their presence in this growing commercial business, CMISA needed a new corporate identity that would serve their needs and reflect their mandate.

This logo is comprised of four elements: a ship; Polaris – the North Star; the Canadian maple leaf; and waves forming the wake. Because the marine sector is a diverse industry, I created a simplified ship silhouette that was designed to be inclusive of all marine vessels such as coast guards, ferries, and commercial, research and military ships.

I included Polaris to honour the history of seafaring when early navigators used the star – an unmoving landmark in the sky – to direct them across the seas. It also serves as a symbol of the way members will confidently navigate the industry, knowing that CMISA is there to guide and assist.

The mounting waves add dimension and represent the opportunity to build, support, sustain and shape the thriving and developing marine and shipbuilding industry.

Finally, the maple leaf was a clear choice to represent the significant benefit the marine industry brings to the country’s economy and decidedly communicate the organization's jurisdiction. We employed it using negative space to create a backdrop for the ship illustration.
