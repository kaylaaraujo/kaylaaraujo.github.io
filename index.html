Rumpelstiltskin Children's Book Spread

The design decisions are based off of symbolism.

In Grimm’s fairytale Rumpelstiltskin, a miller’s daughter is given an impossible task by the king to turn mounds of hay into pure gold. The story title shows a representation of this aspect of the story.

The stacking of the syllables of Rumpelstiltskin represent the number three in the story — three times Rumpelstiltskin turned the straw into gold, the three days the miller’s daughter guessed Rumpelstiltskin’s name, and her three guesses within each of those days.

The Kite Runner: Cover Redesign

I wanted to communicate the story of how the boys’ friendship deteriorated. Winning the kite fights was always their proudest moments and they celebrated those together. It is only until one unfortunate event that things began to unravel.

The image I chose to represent the key element is a kite tail that spells the word Runner with bows at the top and none on the bottom.

The bows that are on the tight winding parts of the rope represent Amir and Hassan’s friendship when they triumphed in competition and before the threats. The number of bows (3) represents the three boys who tormented Amir and Hassan. The section without the bows represents the unraveling of the boys’ lives that led them to end their friendship.

About Me

I’m Kayla, a Graphic Design and Advertising graduate from Algonquin College. I have lived in Ottawa, Canada my whole life and I have been itching to seek new challenges in the world of design.

There’s something intriguing to me about solving a problem visually and coming up with new ways to communicate emotions and ideas. And there are so many ways to create! Design is limitless.

I love simplistic design that is functional, has a clear conceptual idea, and draws the attention of people using its enduring personality.

At the end of the day, I truly believe that life’s problems can always be solved in a beautiful and creative way.
